Mentorship for Alignment Research (MARS)

A part-time programme where you work closely with experienced mentors on concrete AI safety research directions.

What to expect

Structured research with real outputs

MARS pairs small groups with mentors to explore impactful alignment projects. Each cohort starts together in Cambridge before moving into a remote, research-focused rhythm supported by the CAISH team.

We keep the commitment part-time so you can contribute meaningfully alongside studies or work while building the skills and relationships that lead to long-term impact.

Programme Snapshot

  • Format: Part-time, 10–15 hours per week
  • Kickoff: Two weeks in Cambridge with your mentor and cohort
  • Support: Research manager guidance, peer accountability, and CAISH community
  • Outcome: A clear, publishable output that advances your area
Mentors

Learn directly from experienced researchers

Euan McLean

Euan focuses on mechanistic interpretability and adversarial robustness, mentoring teams on building empirical intuitions and writing up results rigorously.

Additional mentors

We match you with other mentors based on your interests—covering governance, evaluations, interpretability, and more—so every team has tailored guidance.

Cohort flow

How the programme runs

Applying

You'll apply to work with a specific mentor on a research area that interests you. We review applications alongside the mentor, and if you're a good fit, there may be a short interview. Once accepted, we'll help you get to Cambridge for the kickoff.

Kickoff week

You'll spend two weeks in Cambridge getting to know your mentor, fellow participants, and the city. The kickoff is designed to build momentum—it's much easier to make progress when you're working alongside others. You'll leave with a clear research direction and the relationships to sustain it.

Remote phase

After the kickoff, you'll continue working remotely with support from your mentor, a research manager, and the CAISH team. Most participants spend 10–15 hours per week on their projects.

Output

We expect you to produce something. For most, that's a paper or blog post—but if you have another way to share impactful work, we'll support it.

Beyond MARS

We provide career support for applying to full-time AI safety roles. You'll also be integrated into the broader Meridian ecosystem, with access to visiting researchers and the wider community.
