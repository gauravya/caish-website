MARS Programme

Mentorship for Alignment Researchers

The window to prepare for AI's most serious risks is limited. We need more people—across disciplines and career stages—who can contribute to safety research.

But breaking into this work is hard, especially if you're balancing study or a full-time job.

MARS makes it possible. We connect aspiring researchers with experienced mentors for part-time, project-based AI safety research. Over a few months, you'll build practical research skills, explore the field, and work on problems that matter.

MARS 5.0 runs in spring 2025, starting with a two-week in-person kickoff in Cambridge. Accommodation and meals provided. After that, you'll continue remotely with your mentor.

What the programme is about

MARS pairs motivated participants with mentors working on high-priority AI safety research. You'll define a clear project, meet regularly, and produce tangible outputs with feedback from your mentor and peers.

Mentorship

Weekly one-on-one calls with an experienced researcher focused on alignment, governance, or control.

Research Project

Work on a concrete research question over the course of the programme, with the goal of producing a publishable output.

Community

Join a cohort of mentees, attend talks, and connect with the broader AI safety research community.
