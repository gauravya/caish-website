MARS Programme

Mentorship for Alignment Researchers

MARS 5.0 is a part-time research programme that pairs aspiring AI safety researchers with experienced mentors. It starts with a two-week in-person kickoff in Cambridge—accommodation and meals provided—where you'll meet your cohort, attend talks and workshops, and get oriented to your project. From there, you continue remotely with your mentor over the following months.

How it works

1

Applying

You'll apply to work with a specific mentor on a research question. We review applications alongside the mentor, and if you're a strong fit, there may be a short interview. Once accepted, we'll help arrange travel to Cambridge for the kickoff week.

→
2

Kickoff

Two weeks in Cambridge with your mentor, fellow participants, and the CAISH team. You'll scope your research project, build momentum, and leave with a clear direction and the relationships to sustain it.

→
3

Remote phase

You'll continue working remotely, around 8 hours per week, with regular check-ins from your mentor and a research manager. We will cover compute costs for your project.

→
4

Final presentation & output

At the end of the programme, you'll present your work. We expect a concrete output: a paper, blog post, or other form that shares your research with the field.

→
5

What comes next

We provide career support for full-time AI safety roles. Strong participants are invited to apply for Meridian's visiting researcher programme or accelerator week, where you can seek grants to continue your work.
