Privacy Policy

Last updated: 27 February 2026

Cambridge AI Safety Hub (CAISH) is a project of Meridian Impact CIC (company number 13653958). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data we collect, why, how we use it, and your rights.

1. What we collect

Meeting bookings

When you book a meeting through our calendar, we collect:

  • Full name and email address — to create the calendar event and send you an invitation
  • Meeting topic — so the host can prepare for your meeting
  • Meeting time, duration, and format (virtual or in-person) — to schedule the event

This data is sent to the host (Gaurav Yadav and/or Justin Dollman) via a calendar invitation and an email notification. Your name, email, and topic will be visible to the host.

Google Calendar overlay (optional)

Our booking page offers an optional feature to overlay your own Google Calendar events so you can see your availability alongside ours. If you choose to use this:

  • We request read-only access to your Google Calendar events for the week being viewed
  • Your calendar data is processed entirely in your browser — it is never sent to our servers, never stored, and never shared
  • We only use it to display your busy times on the booking calendar
  • You can revoke access at any time via your Google account permissions

This feature is entirely optional. The booking system works fully without it.

Mailing list

If you sign up for our mailing list, your email address is collected and stored by HubSpot, our email platform. We use it only to send you updates about CAISH programmes and events.

Programme applications

Applications for our Fellowship and MARS programmes are collected via Airtable. The data you submit (which may include your name, email, background, and research interests) is stored in Airtable and used solely to evaluate your application.

Technical data

  • IP address — used for rate limiting on the booking system to prevent spam. Stored in server memory only and not persisted to disk.
  • Browser storage — we use localStorage to cache availability data (auto-cleared after 24 hours) and sessionStorage to preserve your selection during the booking flow. No tracking or analytics cookies are used.

2. How we use your data

We use your data only for the purpose it was collected:

  • Creating calendar events and sending meeting invitations
  • Notifying the host about upcoming meetings
  • Preventing spam via rate limiting
  • Evaluating programme applications
  • Sending mailing list updates (if you opted in)

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with advertisers. We do not use analytics or tracking pixels on this site.

4. Data retention

  • Calendar events — remain in the host’s Google Calendar. You can request deletion at any time.
  • Email notifications — retained in the host’s email inbox.
  • Rate limiting data — stored in server memory only, cleared on each server restart (typically within hours).
  • Browser cache — auto-cleared after 24 hours. Session data cleared when you close the tab.
  • Mailing list — retained until you unsubscribe.
  • Programme applications — retained for the duration of the application cycle.

5. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct any inaccurate data
  • Delete your data (request erasure)
  • Withdraw consent at any time (e.g. unsubscribe from the mailing list, revoke Google Calendar access)

To exercise any of these rights, email hello@cambridgeaisafety.org.

6. Legal basis for processing (GDPR)

  • Consent — for meeting bookings, mailing list, programme applications, and Google Calendar overlay
  • Legitimate interest — for rate limiting and spam prevention

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “last updated” date at the top will reflect any changes.

8. Contact

If you have questions about this policy or your data, contact us at hello@cambridgeaisafety.org.
