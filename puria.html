Puria Radmard

Co-Director, Geodesic Research

Puria Radmard came up through machine learning: undergrad and master’s at Cambridge, then a PhD in computational neuroscience, with natural language processing (NLP) running through all of it.

Existential risk was a side interest. He’d dabbled, but never connected it to the systems he was actually building. As he puts it, he lacked the imagination. Then conversations at CAISH and Meridian showed him a flourishing AI safety ecosystem built on exactly the NLP systems he knew, and he started getting involved.

In February he paused his PhD, first for two months, then for another six, and went full-time. He’s now co-director of Geodesic Research, alongside Cam Tice and Edward Young, working on alignment pre-training, a largely unowned corner of AI safety that asks how to bake safety into models from the first stages of training, rather than bolting it on at the end.

In this video, he talks about his path in, and the blockers that almost kept him out.
