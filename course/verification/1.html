Unit 01

Why this matters

Start with why advanced AI is dangerous at all, the case for misalignment and the case for extreme concentration of power. Then move to why compute is the lever governments can act on, what a verification strategy looks like once you take the geopolitics seriously, and what a mature verification regime has historically required.

Read the pieces properly. Lean on each one's abstract or summary to decide what you take away, not someone else's gloss.

READ 1.1

Risks from power-seeking AI

80,000 Hours problem profile

The foundational case for why advanced AI could be dangerous at all. The argument runs that we will build AI systems with long-term goals, that such systems have reason to seek power and resist being controlled, and that a capable enough one could disempower humanity. Read it for the motivation underneath the whole course. If misalignment is not a real risk, none of the verification work has any stakes.

READ 1.2

Extreme power concentration

80,000 Hours problem profile

The second reason the stakes are high, and the one people reach for less often. Even if we keep AI under human control, it could let a small group concentrate power to a degree with no historical precedent, by automating away the value of most human labour and handing whoever controls the best systems an overwhelming lead. Read it for the case that verification matters not only against misaligned AI but against people using AI to seize and hold power.
